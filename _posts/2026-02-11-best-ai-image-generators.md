## 1. Midjourney — Best Overall Quality

**Price:** $10-60/month

Midjourney consistently produces the most aesthetically pleasing images. It has a distinct "look" that's hard to replicate.

### Pros
- Stunning artistic quality
- Great at stylized images
- Active community
- Fast generation
- V6 handles text well

### Cons
- Discord-only interface
- Learning curve for prompts
- No API access
- Can't run locally

### Best For
- Marketing visuals
- Concept art
- Social media graphics
- Artistic projects

**Sample prompt:** "A cozy coffee shop in Tokyo during cherry blossom season, warm lighting, Studio Ghibli style --ar 16:9 --v 6"

---

## 2. DALL-E 3 — Best for Beginners

**Price:** Included with ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo)

DALL-E 3's integration with ChatGPT makes it incredibly easy to use. Just describe what you want in natural language.

### Pros
- Natural language prompts
- ChatGPT integration
- Good text rendering
- Easy to iterate
- No prompt engineering needed

### Cons
- Less artistic than Midjourney
- Limited style control
- Can't generate certain content
- Slower than competitors

### Best For
- Quick mockups
- Blog images
- Presentations
- Non-designers

---

## 3. Stable Diffusion — Best for Power Users

**Price:** Free (run locally) or various hosted options

Stable Diffusion is open-source and infinitely customizable. If you want full control, this is it.

### Pros
- Completely free (local)
- Unlimited generations
- Custom models (LoRAs)
- Full control
- No content restrictions
- Privacy (runs locally)

### Cons
- Requires good GPU
- Technical setup
- Steeper learning curve
- Base model less polished

### Best For
- Developers
- Custom workflows
- High-volume generation
- Specific styles (via LoRAs)

**Recommended setup:** ComfyUI + SDXL + custom LoRAs

### Pros

- Great anatomy

- Open weights available

### Cons

- Newer (less community resources)

### Best For
- Product photography
- Stock photos

## Tips for Better Results

1. **Be specific** — "A golden retriever" vs "A golden retriever puppy playing in autumn leaves, soft afternoon light"

2. **Specify style** — Add "digital art", "oil painting", "photograph", etc.

3. **Use aspect ratios** — Match your intended use (16:9 for YouTube, 1:1 for Instagram)

4. **Iterate** — Generate multiple versions and refine

5. **Learn from others** — Study prompts that produce good results
